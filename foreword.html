Foreword

A Family Story

December 2025

The whole experience of writing our family story has been a journey. It began as a Covid project in March 2020, inspired by the words of Chris Hadfield, a Canadian astronaut and one of our heroes.

In a video posted online, Chris compared Covid self-isolation to his experience in space, and he shared some tips to not only cope with it, but thrive.

His advice was four-fold: Know your risks, define your mission, take action to start accomplishing it, and take care of yourself and others.

When we applied this advice to ourselves, we first identified the risks of the disease itself and made it our mission to stay healthy and safe through what was a deadly pandemic for so many.

We also identified the need to support each other and strengthen our connections with family and friends. We were living in Edmonton when the pandemic broke out, Joanna was in Ottawa, and Allan and his family were in Uppsala, Sweden.

When Covid took that away, we turned to regular phone chats and virtual family get-togethers on Zoom most weekends. Not nearly the same as being together in person, but those calls and Zooms meant a lot.

With so much time on our hands, we decided on another action, and that was to review our family archives — all those photographs, audio and video tapes, and documents we had been saving over the years.

We had to put the project on hold when we moved back to Ottawa in 2021. By the time we returned to our project in earnest, a few years had gone by.

“Your story is what you have, what you will always have. It is something to own.”
— Michelle Obama, Becoming

In spring 2024, we connected with a writer named Lana Okerlund, and her interviewing, writing, and creative skills reinvigorated us.

The whole experience of producing our story has been an opportunity to explore, savour, and understand our lives.

As Pericles said, “What you leave behind is not what is engraved in stone monuments, but what is woven into the lives of others.”

We are profoundly grateful to our parents, our children, and our grandchildren for the loving family tapestry we continue to create together.

— Bill and Marie
December 2025
